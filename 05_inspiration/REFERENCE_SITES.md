# Reference Sites — Interloom Redesign
> Compiled: May 6, 2026

Client note: *"Son estilos súper distintos, pero ambos han hecho un buen trabajo visual en sus webs que nos podrían servir como referencias y bench para la nuestra y las imágenes que usemos."*

---

## 1. Cono Group — Sustainability Page
**URL:** https://www.cono-group.com/es/sustentabilidad  
**What the client said:** "Me pareció buena en general, le cambiaría los párrafos."

### What to borrow
- Clean, confident typography — large serif heading over full-width hero image
- Well-proportioned white space between sections
- Consistent nav: simple top bar, logo left, links right, language toggle
- Image usage: full-bleed editorial photography (soil, nature) without feeling cliché
- Grid of photos for storytelling (team, field, operations)
- Breadcrumb navigation for subpages

### What to avoid
- Long blocks of body text without visual breaks (client specifically mentioned this)
- Text-heavy sustainability copy with no visual rhythm

## 2. LDC — Product Page
**URL:** https://www.ldc.com/product/animal-feed-pet-food/  
**What the client said:** "La parte de producto me pareció buena." *(they liked the product section — exact aspect TBD, confirm with Mazor)*

### What's on the page (observed)
- Left sidebar navigation for product categories
- 2-column product listing: small product image left + name + short description right
- Clear typographic hierarchy: section intro headline → category → product name → description
- Minimal color — white background, dark text, subtle accent
- Full-bleed photo header with text overlay

### ⚠️ Note
Client liked the product section but didn't specify what exactly. Could be the layout, the product image style, the copy format, or the category structure. Worth a quick clarification with Mazor before we commit to a direction here.

### What to avoid
- Very corporate/cold global enterprise feel — Interloom should feel more human and connected to origin

## 3. Globe Natural Superfoods
**URL:** https://globenatural.com/  
**What the client said:** Style reference.

### What to borrow
- Warm, natural aesthetic — off-white/cream backgrounds, organic logo mark
- Soft color palette that feels earthy without being rustic
- Category-based nav: Inicio, Nosotros, Servicios, Productos, Sostenibilidad, Contacto — mirrors what Interloom needs
- Texture use: subtle background textures that feel premium

### Notes
- Site loads slowly — hero is animation-heavy. Keep Interloom lean and fast.

## 4. Peruvian Nature
**URL:** https://peruviannature.com/  
**What the client said:** Style reference.

### What to borrow
- Bold hero with confident full-viewport color block + large typography
- Contrast between dark navy header/nav and light content sections
- More editorial/design-forward than the other references
- "Superfoods" framing — elevated product positioning
- Bilingual (ESP | ENG) — same requirement as Interloom
- Structured nav: Nosotros, Superfoods (dropdown), Servicios, Sostenibilidad, Contacto, Blog

### Notes
- The dark navy + lime green combo is quite bold and trendy — too far from Interloom's brand
- But the **confidence and editorial quality** of the design is directionally right
- Their product section execution is worth studying

## Synthesis: What This Means for Interloom

Combining the client's references with the brand principles:

**The direction is:** Cono Group's structure and confidence + LDC's product layout logic + Globe Natural's warmth + Peruvian Nature's editorial boldness — all filtered through Interloom's specific palette (white-dominant, forest green accents, warm brown secondary, Playfair + Lexend typography).

### Design Principles for Our Approach
- **White-dominant** (80%+ per brand manual) with generous space
- **Typography-led sections** — let Playfair Display do the heavy lifting for section headings
- **Full-bleed photography** for heroes and section dividers — but clean shots, not busy
- **Division cards** with clear visual identity for each business unit
- **Left-nav or tab-based product navigation** (LDC model) for the Divisiones section
- **WhatsApp CTA button** for DEL CAMPO (per Mazor's requirement)
- Minimal plugin reliance — static/CSS hero instead of LayerSlider
- Fast load times — no heavy sliders or animations
